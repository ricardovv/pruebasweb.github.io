ALMA

The Atacama Large Millimeter/submillimeter Array (ALMA) is a revolutionary instrument operating in the very thin and dry air of northern Chile’s Atacama desert, at an altitude of 5,000 meters above sea level. ALMA is composed of an array of 66 high-precision antennas working together at the millimeter and submillimeter wavelengths, corresponding to frequencies from about 30 to 950 GHz. These 7 and 12 meters parabolic antennas, extremely precise in surface accuracy, can be moved around on the high altitude plateau to provide different array configurations, ranging in size from about 150 meters up to 15 kilometers. The ALMA Observatory is an international partnership between Europe, North America, and Japan, in cooperation with the Republic of Chile.1
